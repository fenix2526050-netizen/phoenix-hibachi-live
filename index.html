Booking Rules & FAQ

Before guests arrive.

What do customers need to provide?

A clean outdoor cooking area, enough seating, access to water/trash, parking or unloading access, and enough space for the chef and grill setup.

Do you serve NY, NJ, CT, and Long Island?

Yes. Travel fees may apply depending on route, parking, tolls, and chef availability.

Are kids half price?

Yes, kids under 12 are half price. The event still has a 10-person minimum charge.

Can we add sushi or noodles?

Yes. Sushi trays, sashimi combo, gyoza, edamame, and noodle trays can be added to the hibachi package.

Can you handle allergies?

Allergies must be disclosed before booking. High-risk allergies need manager confirmation before the order is accepted.

What happens if it rains or timing changes?

The booking popup asks for a rain backup, parking/unloading details, and notes. Outdoor cooking requires a safe covered area when needed. Chef may arrive early for setup, but the event is based on the confirmed service time. If guests are late, timing should be discussed directly with the chef; the chef may still begin prep or service on schedule when another booking follows.

What is the 72-hour cancellation policy?

Customers may cancel more than 72 hours before the event for manager review. Within 72 hours of the scheduled event, the deposit is non-refundable and the booking can only be rescheduled, subject to chef availability and route confirmation.

Terms & Waiver

Phoenix Hibachi booking terms.

Please review the full policy before submitting your booking request. These terms explain setup, deposit, cancellation, allergy, media, and service expectations for Phoenix Hibachi events.

1. Booking request and confirmation

Submitting this form creates a booking request, not a guaranteed reservation. Phoenix Hibachi must review chef availability, exact address, route timing, parking, weather, setup space, allergies, special requests, and deposit status before the event is finally accepted.

2. What Phoenix Hibachi provides

Phoenix Hibachi provides the private hibachi chef, portable grill, standard cooking tools, selected proteins, fried rice, hibachi vegetables, sauces, and the live hibachi cooking experience for the selected package and confirmed guest count.

3. Host responsibilities

The host is responsible for guest tables, chairs, plates, utensils, napkins, trash bags, safe access to the cooking area, parking or unloading access, and keeping children, pets, and guests away from the hot grill and chef work area.

4. Setup, safety and weather

The cooking area must be safe, level, ventilated, and protected from unsafe weather. Phoenix Hibachi may refuse, pause, move, or reschedule service if wind, rain, snow, lightning, poor ventilation, unsafe flooring, blocked access, or crowd behavior creates risk for guests, staff, or property.

5. Event time, chef setup and guest delays

The event is scheduled around the confirmed service time. A chef may arrive earlier to unload, set up, and prepare the cooking station, but early arrival does not automatically move the party start time earlier. If the host wants to start earlier or later, the host may discuss it with the chef. If the host or guests are not ready by the scheduled time, Phoenix Hibachi and the assigned chef may begin setup, prep, or service according to the confirmed schedule because the chef may have other appointments, route commitments, or staffing obligations the same day.

6. Special party requests

Phoenix Hibachi provides hibachi cooking, food service, and family-friendly entertainment. Any special request outside normal service — such as asking the chef to wear a costume, join a themed party moment, or be pushed/tossed into a pool — must be discussed before the event and approved by Phoenix Hibachi and the assigned chef.

This allows the chef to prepare properly, including bringing a change of clothes if needed, and to confirm the request is safe for cooking and service.

All special requests must be safe, respectful, voluntary, and appropriate. Phoenix Hibachi and the assigned chef may refuse or stop any activity that may cause injury, clothing damage, equipment damage, disrespect, or schedule delays. The host and guests accept responsibility for any risks, injuries, damages, or losses resulting from these special activities.

7. Travel fee and minimum guests

A default travel fee may apply. Longer routes, tolls, paid parking, difficult access, far-distance events, or additional chef requests may increase the final travel fee. Phoenix Hibachi may require a higher minimum guest count for distant locations, peak dates, or events requiring additional staffing.

8. Add-ons and menu changes

Add-ons such as sushi trays, appetizers, noodles, drinks, or special items are subject to availability. Quantity changes, menu changes, and headcount changes requested within 7 days of the event may require price adjustment and manager approval.

9. Allergies and dietary restrictions

Customers must disclose allergies and dietary restrictions before the event. Because hibachi service uses shared equipment, sauces, cooking surfaces, and transport containers, Phoenix Hibachi cannot guarantee an allergen-free environment. Guests with severe allergies should contact management before booking and may need to avoid consuming certain items.

10. Food handling and leftovers

Food is prepared for the confirmed event time and standard restaurant-style portions. Once food is served, the host is responsible for safe storage and handling of leftovers. Phoenix Hibachi is not responsible for food left out too long, improperly stored, reheated, or consumed after the event.

11. Cancellation, reschedule and deposit

Deposits are used to reserve chef time, prep work, route planning, and ingredients. Cancellations made more than 72 hours before the event may be reviewed by management. Within 72 hours of the event, deposits are non-refundable and the available remedy is manager-approved rescheduling, subject to availability.

12. Promotional photos and videos

Phoenix Hibachi may occasionally take photos or short videos during events for business promotion, training, website content, social media, and portfolio use. By submitting a booking request, the host confirms that Phoenix Hibachi may capture and use event photos or videos unless the host tells Phoenix Hibachi in writing before the event that photos or videos are not allowed. The host is responsible for informing guests of any photo or video preference. If there are privacy concerns, minors, private guests, or any no-filming request, please notify Phoenix Hibachi before the event so the team can respect it.

13. Liability waiver

By booking, the customer understands that live hibachi service involves heat, open flame, oil, smoke, sharp tools, hot surfaces, food allergens, outdoor conditions, guest behavior, and any customer-requested entertainment activity. To the fullest extent permitted by law, the customer agrees to hold Phoenix Hibachi, its owners, contractors, chefs, and staff harmless from claims arising from unsafe setup, inaccurate information, guest behavior, pets, children, property conditions, weather, parking/access issues, undisclosed allergies, or extra activities requested by the host or guests.

14. Final agreement

By checking the agreement box and submitting the request, the customer confirms that the information provided is accurate, the host accepts these rules, and Phoenix Hibachi may contact the customer by phone, text, or email to confirm details, collect deposit, and finalize the event.
